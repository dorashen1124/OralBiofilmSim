# 3D-Computational-Model-of-Human-Mouth-to-Simulate-Oral-Behavior-and-Biofilm-Formation
My code helps to explore and visualize how some prevalent species of bacteria move and interact in a oral microbiome environment. 

It's not a comprehensive model for bacteria but this project crossed multi platform (pycharm, blender, and r studio) to extract and visualize data from the curatedMetagenomicData megaset. 

The reason I want to develop this project is because I'm interested in dentistry and was curious about how does the cavities formed. Having a cavity and went dental check up with dentists used to be my nightmare and I was traumatized by it. I want to further explore this intriguing oral microbiome system about what activities could happened inside. The oral microbiome is a highly diverse ecosystem consists bacteria (my main focus), fungi, archaea, viruses, and protozoa all influencing oral and systemic health. They all have the ability to form structured communities, which is called biofilms, in different parts of mouth. 

#Key Findings from the literal search
-There are more than 700+ of bacterial species which the individuals share similar species but differ in abundance and strains. 

-Different mouth regions have different microbial communities and the biofilms (big communities) have complex spatial organization (having changes in interactions and disease).

-The structure of biofilm influences bacteria's nutrient exchange, growth, and disease development.

-The formation of cavities was caused by acid producing, biofilm-forming microbes and community imbalance (dysbiosis)
    -There are some gum disease relating with microbial changes and immune imbalance such as periodontal disease which will cause inflammation and bone loss 

-Some scientific advances included metagenomics and imaging which provides better identification of microbes understanding of spatial structure (which is the goal of my research project)
